Water meters are cheap and available at most hardware stores. I got mine for a little over $5.

The slow but reliable way to find plants that work in the place you want them and with the care you'll give them is to buy them small. Repot regularly and toss out the plants that are too finicky (ie look sickly or died). After a while, you will probably observe that plants that require certain conditions are or aren't for you.

Watch out for small trees made bushy by lots of tree in one pot. When they grow, they will be too crowded and the roots will be entangled. Remove and repot one stem and for a few years, it will be a pathetic little seedling and the other plant will be bushy, but at some time your tiny hibiscus will be a nice tree and your bushy plant will be 5 trees fighting for room, none of them doing well.

Winter flowering plants, like Christmas or Easter cactus, often require short days to trigger a blooming period. If they get plenty of artificial light in the evenings, they will not know it is time to flower. Either stick them in a spare room, where they will get daylight only or stick a bucket over them at 4 or 5 pm.
